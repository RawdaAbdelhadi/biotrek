The Rodent Research-23 OSD-665

Biological experiments conducted in space offer invaluable insights into how life adapts to extraterrestrial environments. However, the complexity of these experiments often hinders their accessibility to a broader scientific community

Simple Steps to Achieve goal

Read the passage before starting the questions

Sending mice to space: Scientists used 20 mice aged 16 to 17 weeks. The mice were sent to the ISS aboard the SpaceX-21 mission, living in a microgravity environment for 38 days in a specially designed rodent habitat. Returning mice to Earth: On January 13, 2021, the mice were returned alive to Earth, landing in the ocean. They were then transported to the Kennedy Space Center and later to Texas A&M University for study. Post-return study of mice: The scientists analyzed tissue samples from the mice after humanely euthanizing them to collect the necessary samples. The mice were euthanized between January 14 and 20, 2021. Dividing the mice into three groups: Flight Group: Mice that traveled to space. Habitat Ground Control Group: Mice that remained on Earth but in space-simulated conditions. Vivarium Ground Control Group: Mice that lived in completely normal conditions on Earth.
