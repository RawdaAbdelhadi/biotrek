The Rodent Research-23 experiment is a scientific study conducted on the International Space Station (ISS) aimed at understanding the effects of microgravity on eye health and the associated blood vessels. In this study, 20 male mice were sent to space to examine changes in the structure and function of the arteries, veins, and lymphatic vessels in the eyes after being exposed to the space environment for 38 days.
